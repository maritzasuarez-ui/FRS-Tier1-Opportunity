FRS A∧B∧C Resolution Rubric — Tag Reference

A contact is Resolved (verdict = 1) only when ALL THREE criteria pass simultaneously
A ∧ B ∧ C
The three-criteria rubric — all must pass for FRS = Resolved
A
Was the issue addressed?
PASS ✓
The expert engaged with the customer's issue and provided guidance or a solution.
FAIL ✗
Issue was acknowledged but not addressed, or the call ended before the expert could engage.
AND
B
Is anything still pending?
PASS ✓
No open dependencies remain — no pending Intuit action, customer action, or external process.
FAIL ✗
A blocker remains: a follow-up needed, a transfer required, or the customer must still act.
AND
C
Did the customer understand the outcome?
PASS ✓
The customer confirmed understanding — they acknowledged next steps or the resolution. Did not challenge the expert or show disapproval.
FAIL ✗
No confirmation from customer. They may have ended the call confused or without clarity.
Contact & Domain follow the same rubric. Domain (TTL and FS) has a few nuances on overlay credits.
Methodology notes
Primary tag is selected by priority hierarchy. A contact that fails both B and C only gets one tag — whichever ranks highest in the V2 hierarchy. This mapping tells you the primary reason each tag represents, not the only verdict it could fail.

Some assignments are judgment calls. Example: customer_refuses_steps could be argued as A-FAIL (no real engagement) or C-FAIL (rejected the outcome). It is placed in B because there was a path forward that the customer chose not to complete. Similarly, transfer_recommended could be overlay-credited as Resolved (good transfer) — bucketed here as B because the contact itself did not close.

Overlay-credited tags exist outside this framework. Three tags (customer_action_required, multi_issue_flag_partial_res, good_transfer_recommended) can be marked Resolved via overlay even though they fail B. Those overlays apply on top of the A+B+C rubric.

Total count: 32 V2 tags. 11 A-FAIL + 16 B-FAIL + 4 C-FAIL + 1 Other. (multi_issue_flag_partial_res and multi_issue_flag_no_res are V1 synthetic tags; V2 redistributes multi-issue contacts to underlying real tags so they are not separately bucketed here.)
