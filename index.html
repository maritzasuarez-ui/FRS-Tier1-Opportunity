📖 Definitions & Rubric — Friction Layer Reference

A∧B∧C rubric · 6 friction layers · All friction tasks with FRS% and action owner
A∧B∧C Resolution Rubric
BUCKET A — Issue not addressed
Contact ended before meaningful engagement. The customer issue was never reached — they abandoned, call dropped, misrouted, or connection failed before the agent could help.

Cannot be coached away. Requires IVR redesign, callback triggers, routing logic fix, channel workstream.

Key friction tasks: Routing/misrouting · Dropped call/connection · Agent system/tools
BUCKET B — Something still pending
Issue engaged but resolution needs follow-up. Product failure, pending process, fund holds, system restrictions, or policy blocks mean the contact cannot close as resolved this session.

Dominant lever — 86.3% of friction contacts. Product and ops fix, not coaching.

Key friction tasks: Payroll & tax · Billing · Identity & RBAC · Desktop · Fund holds · Account holds
BUCKET C — Customer did not confirm
Steps taken but customer expressed confusion or transcript lacked confirmation. Expert resolved or attempted to resolve, but clarity or closing technique fell short.

Most directly coachable. 5.1% of contacts — smallest lever but immediate response to coaching.

Key friction tasks: Expert knowledge/accuracy · Tax automation confusion
